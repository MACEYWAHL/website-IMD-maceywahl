Bonding

Bunnies are prey animals who are naturally docile and scared animals. To earn their trust and create a bond with them is a harder and longer process than with a dog or cat. Here are some starting tip & tricks to get your bunny to love and trust you.

Petting

Most rabbits loving being pet and having a good massage. Petting your rabbit is one of the easiest ways to bond with them. It gets them used to hands being on them which makes all handling easier and safer for your bunny. Many rabbits will bow their head to show you they want pets.

Hand Feeding

Hand feeding is a great way to build trust with your bunny. It builds a positive association with you and food. This can teach them not to bite and that coming up to you is beneficial for them. Treats should be given in moderation but are a great way to bond with your bun.

Sitting with Bun

Getting on the floor next to your bunny is a great first step to bonding. Don't immediately try to pet a new bunny as they might get scared of you. Getting on the same level makes you seem less like a dangerous predator. When your bunny approaches you, let them sniff you and run away. Let beginning interaction be on their terms.

There are so many ways to bond with your bunny. Bonding is important to make your bunny feel safe and happy. There is nothing better than getting the trust of a prey animal!

Behavior

Bunnies have interesting behavior and their behavior can tell you a lot about how they feel or what they want. With bunnies, it is incredibly important to know these as some of them or lack of certain beahviors could point to an illness.

Binkies

Binking is a rabbit's form of zoomies. They'll sprint around, kick their feet, and jump into the air. If your rabbit does this, it means they are happy and feel safe, it also gets out extra energy they may have.

Thumping

This is when the rabbit will stomp its back foot on the ground. This means your rabbit is upset or frightened; most the time it's when they are mad.

"The Flop"

This is when a rabbit lays on their side exposing the belly and puts their head on the ground. Many rabbits will sleep in these positions. If your rabbit does this around you, it means they are comfortable around you and do not see you as a predator. You have gained their trust!

"The Loaf"

The "loaf" is when a rabbit tucks their legs and feet underneath the body and sits with just its body showing. This is a position many rabbits will rest and even sleep in. Your rabbit could also be trying to conserve it's body heat.

Growls/Grunts

Yes it may be surprising but bunnies actually do make sound. Many bunnies will grunt or growl when they are being territorial, don't like what your doing, or has some food aggression. This sound is kind of a warning sign before a bite or a thump if they feel threatened.

Eating their Poop

Rabbits will often eat their own poop. It is a specific kind of poop they eat called a cecotroph. This is a more wet version of their normal poop and they eat it for extra nutrients; so, don't fret if you see your bunny doing this, it is healthy!
